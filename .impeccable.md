## Design Context

### Users
Dutch SMB entrepreneurs (broad audience), typically 30–55, looking to hire a professional web designer for their business. They visit on desktop and mobile, often after seeing referrals or searching "website laten bouwen Amsterdam/Uithoorn". Decision-makers comparing options — they want proof of quality and trust before reaching out.

### Brand Personality
**Three words:** Vakmanschap · Directheid · Durf (Craftsmanship · Directness · Boldness)
Peter is a solo craftsman — not an agency. One person who builds everything himself. The brand should feel confident, no-nonsense, and distinctly personal. Not corporate, not trendy, not safe.

### Aesthetic Direction
**Brutalist.** Hard 2px borders, pure black/white/#EA2525 red, zero border-radius, offset box-shadows, mono labels.
- **Fonts (confirmed by client):** Archivo Black (display/headings), Space Grotesk (body), JetBrains Mono (mono/labels)
- **Colors (confirmed):** `--black: #000`, `--white: #fff`, `--red: #EA2525`, `--near-black: #0A0A0A`, `--bone: #F5F5F5`, `--muted: #929292`
- **Theme:** Light (white primary background) with dark sections for contrast
- **Anti-references:** No softness, no rounded corners, no gradients on text, no glassmorphism, no agency polish

### Design Principles
1. **Craft is visible** — every detail signals that a human, not a template, made this
2. **Red is a weapon** — #EA2525 used sparingly as the only accent, never diluted
3. **Borders over shadows** — hard 2px black borders instead of soft shadows for structure
4. **Typography as layout** — large Archivo Black headlines do visual heavy lifting
5. **Direct CTAs** — one clear action per section, never buried or ambiguous
